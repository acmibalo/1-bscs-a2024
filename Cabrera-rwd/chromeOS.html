ChromeOS

ChromeOS is an operating system developed by Google for Chromebooks, based on the Linux kernel. It focuses on web applications and cloud computing, with most tasks handled through the Chrome browser. Known for its speed, simplicity, and security, ChromeOS features automatic updates and built-in virus protection, making it ideal for users who rely on internet-based services.

ChromeOS became popular after its 2011 release, particularly with affordable Chromebooks aimed at students and educators. Its simplicity, speed, and security appealed to users focused on web-based tasks, while the rise of cloud computing further drove adoption, especially in schools.
