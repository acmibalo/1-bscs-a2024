macOS

macOS is Apple’s operating system for Mac computers, developed by Apple Inc., which was co-founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne. It’s known for its user-friendly interface, stability, and security, integrating well with other Apple devices and supporting various applications, featuring tools like Siri and Continuity for an enhanced user experience.

macOS gained popularity after the 2001 launch of Mac OS X, which combined the classic Mac OS with a Unix-based architecture, improving performance and stability. Its attractive Aqua interface and user-friendly features drew in creative professionals and everyday users, with updates like the App Store in 2008 further boosting its appeal.
