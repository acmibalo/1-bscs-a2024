The official currency of the Philippines is the Philippine Peso (â‚±), with the currency code PHP. One peso is equivalent to 100 centavos (or sentimos). Foreign currency can be exchanged at hotels, large department stores, banks, and authorized money-changing outlets. It's important to note that exchanging money outside of these authorized venues is illegal, and the laws surrounding currency exchange are strictly enforced. Most large establishments, including stores, restaurants, hotels, and resorts, accept major credit cards such as American Express, Visa, and MasterCard. Traveler's checks, especially those issued by American Express, are also accepted at hotels and large department stores. However, personal checks drawn on foreign banks are generally not accepted. For the most convenient and seamless transactions, it is recommended to carry the local peso when traveling in the Philippines.
