🌸About Us🌸


🌸 The Story Behind Petal Journey 🌸

Petal Journey, or "Hana no Tabi," was born out of a deep love and admiration for cherry blossoms and the timeless beauty they represent. Our journey began with a group of friends who shared a common passion: the awe-inspiring sakura blooms that paint Japan and many other parts of the world in stunning shades of pink and white each spring.

🌸 Inspirations and Beginnings 🌸

Our founder, Akane Lily, grew up dreaming of one day experiencing the magical hanami festivals in Japan. Living in a place where cherry blossoms were a rare sight, Akane would spend hours watching documentaries and reading books about the vibrant sakura season. These blossoms became a symbol of hope and inspiration for her—a reminder that beauty can be found even in the fleeting moments of life.

🌸 The Birth of a Community 🌸

The spark for Petal Journey ignited when Akane realized that she was not alone in her admiration for sakura. She noticed how people from around the world shared her dream of experiencing the cherry blossoms in full bloom. Inspired by this shared passion, Akane envisioned a platform that could bring people closer to the beauty of sakura, no matter where they were.

Determined to turn this vision into reality, Akane gathered a diverse team of photographers, writers, and tech enthusiasts who shared her passion. Together, they launched Petal Journey—a space where sakura enthusiasts could come together, share their experiences, learn, and inspire each other. Akane believed that by creating this community, she could help others achieve their dream of witnessing the splendor of cherry blossoms, just as she hoped to one day.

In every petal, there's a story. At Petal Journey, we strive to tell these stories and help others create their own. Whether you're planning a trip to Japan, looking to grow your own cherry blossoms, or simply wanting to bask in the beauty of these flowers, we're here to guide and inspire you every step of the way.

🌸 Cherry Blossom Varieties 🌸


  • Japanese Cherry Blossom Tree (Prunus Serrulata)

    The Japanese Cherry Blossom Tree is also called the Sakura tree. In the early to mid-spring season, it produces stunning pink blossoms. The tree has green leaves that, in the fall, turn a vivid scarlet or a warm bronze. These trees have an upright height of 25 feet with a spreading crown. They grow best in full sunlight, with moist, well-drained soil.

  • Yoshino Cherry Blossom Tree (Prunus yedoensis)

    One of the more well-known cherry blossom trees due to its white and pale pink blossoms is the Yoshino cherry blossom tree, also called the Somei-Yoshino tree. In addition to these beautiful flowers, the tree features a vase-shaped crown and exquisite reddish-orange foliage that changes from green in the fall. Before planting one, make sure you have enough space in your yard because these trees grow quickly and reach a maximum height of 30 feet. It's important to remember that the tree's hard cherries are inedible, but non the summer they will draw birds to your yard.

  • Kwanzan Cherry Blossom Tree (Prunus Serrulata)

    In the cherry blossom family, the Kwanzan cherry blossom tree is regarded as the most ornamental. With spreading growth that resembles a vase, the flowering tree reaches a height of thirty feet. In the fall, it turns a lovely yellowish-orange color before lowering itself gradually to the ground. But this tree doesn't bear any fruit.

  • Bird Cherry Blossom Tree (Prunus padus)

    Bird Cherry Blossom Trees are beautiful. Immediately following the onset of the spring season and the emergence of leaves, clusters of fragrant white flowers bloom. The European Bird Cherry Tree is a fruit-bearing tree. But because of their bitterness, the pea-sized cherries that grow on this tree are unfit for human consumption. The tree's lengthy leaves change from green to a lovely yellow in the fall, and the cherries are black.

  • Okame Cherry Tree (Prunus X incam)

    When in bloom, the five-petaled blossoms of the Okame Cherry Tree have a lovely reddish-pink hue. The crowns of these small, upright trees resemble vases and get rounder as they get older, making them ideal for small backyards. Early spring until late autumn is when they bloom.

  • Fuji Cherry Blossom Tree (Prunus incisa)

    The development of Fuji Cherry Blossom Trees is rounded and their foliage is abundant. These trees are little to slightly medium trees that are ideal for a small yard with lots of sunlight because they can reach a height of 26 feet. In the fall, the oval-shaped, serrated leaves of this tree turn a lovely shade of yellow or orange.

  • Sargent’s Cherry Blossom Tree (Prunus sargentii)

    Masses of fragrant, light-pink blooms adorn the Japanese cherry tree known as Sargent's Cherry Tree. It is also known as the North Japanese Cherry Tree and reaches a maximum height of 40 feet. The tree will have red buds in the early spring, which will later develop into pink blooms with wavy borders. This tree is the ideal choice for finding shade on a hot summer day because of its glossy mahogany bark and the way it spreads out.

  • Dwarf Flowering Fuji Cherry Tree (Prunus incisa)

    Dwarf Flowering Fuji Cherry Trees are trees that only reach eight-foot-tall at maturity, making them perfect for yards that have little room for trees. This mini tree can be identified with ease by its bunches of individual white flowers and the fact that its branches are in a zig-zag pattern. The leaves of the tree are green during the summer months, then turn to a gorgeous red as the autumn season approaches.

  • Weeping Higan Cherry Blossom Tree (Prunus subhirtella)

    Weeping Cherry Blossom Trees such as the Weeping Higan Cherry Blossom Tree have branches that droop as if they’re weeping. This particular tree features green leaves and double pink flowers. It also grows up to 30 feet, so it is best suited for a larger yard. The green leaves of this weeping cherry blossom tree turn a golden yellow color as autumn rears its head.

  • Double Weeping Cherry Tree (Prunus X subhirtella)

    The Double Weeping Cherry Tree is an amazing sight for anyone to see in a garden, so if you have room, you’ll want to invest in planting one soon. Topping out at 25 feet, the branches drooping from this tree are amazing and gorgeous. In the middle of spring, the cascading branches of this tree burst forth with dual flowers in a few different varieties of pink.

🌸 History and Cultural Significance 🌸


Cherry blossoms, known as "sakura" in Japan, have a rich history and hold significant cultural importance in many countries. These delicate flowers symbolize the fleeting nature of life and beauty. The tradition of hanami, or flower viewing, dates back over a thousand years to the Heian period in Japan, when the elite would celebrate the blooming of cherry blossoms with poetry and feasts. Today, hanami is enjoyed by people of all backgrounds, with picnics and gatherings under the blossoming trees becoming a cherished springtime activity.



Beyond Japan, cherry blossoms are celebrated in many cultures around the world. In China, they represent love and the feminine mystique. In Korea, the blooming of cherry blossoms is a reminder of the transience of life and is often associated with new beginnings and renewal. The United States also has a deep connection with cherry blossoms, especially in Washington D.C., where the annual National Cherry Blossom Festival commemorates the 1912 gift of 3,000 cherry trees from the Mayor of Tokyo to the city. This event symbolizes the enduring friendship between the two nations.
