The Philippines, officially known as the Republic of the Philippines, is a vibrant archipelagic nation in Southeast Asia. Nestled in the western Pacific Ocean, the country comprises an astonishing 7,641 islands, collectively spanning approximately 300,000 square kilometers. These islands are grouped into three primary geographical divisions: Luzon in the north, the Visayas in the center, and Mindanao in the south. Geographically strategic, the Philippines is surrounded by major bodies of water: the South China Sea to the west, the Philippine Sea to the east, and the Celebes Sea to the south. It also shares maritime borders with several neighboring countries, including Taiwan to the north, Japan to the northeast, Palau to the east and southeast, Indonesia to the south, Malaysia to the southwest, Vietnam to the west, and China to the northwest. As the world's twelfth-most-populous country, the Philippines is a cultural melting pot, boasting a rich tapestry of ethnicities, languages, and traditions. Its capital, Manila, serves as the political and historical heart of the nation, while Quezon City, the most populous city, thrives as a bustling hub of commerce and development.
